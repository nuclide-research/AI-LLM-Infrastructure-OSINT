THESIS NOTE
------------------------------------------------------------
ComfyUI ships zero-auth (no AUTH config flags at all). The auth-on-default
thesis predicts ~100%-unauth of LIVE deployments. Observed: 77.5% of LIVE
hosts unauthenticated. The 22.5% protected are operator-fronted (reverse
proxy, Cloudflare Access, basic-auth wrapper) — i.e. the protection comes
from the operator, never from the platform. Strong thesis confirmation.

INTERPRETATION
--------------
The 175k alt-port population is a CLOUD-PROXY tier — RunPod, Vast.ai, Modal,
AWS direct, A100 ROW Inc, etc. The Shodan title-match catches the proxy's
welcome page, not the ComfyUI binary. Direct IP:port probing from outside
returns the proxy's HTML catchall (650 fp_not_json) or auth-gated 401 (120),
not the platform's /system_stats JSON.

This is consistent with Insight #15 (the ~50% rule) at extreme scale — at
alt-port the title-substring FP rate is ~99%, not ~50%.

Practical implication for the auth-on-default thesis: the alt-port hosts ARE
unauthenticated at the platform layer (ComfyUI has no auth) but are
PROTECTED by the cloud proxy tier (auth-gated 401 or routing isolation).
Protection comes from the substrate, never from the platform — the SAME
operator-side-protection pattern observed on the default-port 22.5% subset.

The thesis still holds: the platform itself remains 100% auth-off. Whether
each end host is reachable depends on the proxy contract, not the platform.
