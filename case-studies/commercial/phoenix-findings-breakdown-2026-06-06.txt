RESEARCH CONTRIBUTION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Third same-day platform survey (Langfuse + RAGFlow + Phoenix).
Phoenix differs from the other two: where Langfuse / RAGFlow expose
only a registration flag, Phoenix exposes the data layer directly
(projects + users). Maps to OWASP LLM02:2025 Sensitive Information
Disclosure (jumped #6 → #2 in 2025 revision due to incidents of this
exact class).

Three-platform corpus rate: Langfuse 88.9% / RAGFlow 87.2% / Phoenix
74.5% — strong empirical baseline for the auth-permissive-default
cohort hypothesis (Candidate Insight #76).

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FP CATALOG
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. http.title:"Phoenix" returns 4,920 hits including Phoenix Framework
   (Erlang/Elixir web framework) and Phoenix Contact (industrial
   networking gear). MUST use port:6006 specifier to scope to Arize
   Phoenix. Insight: title-only dorks for common product names need
   port + content disambiguation.

2. PROJECTS_UNAUTH with `default` project only and 0 users on a host
   may indicate an idle/abandoned Phoenix install — still a finding
   (operator deployed and forgot) but lower severity than active
   deployments with named projects.

3. USERS_UNAUTH count of 2 appears uniformly across hosts. This may
   be Phoenix's default "system" + "admin" account pair, not real
   end-user accounts. Schema verification was attempted but blocked
   by NuClide restraint policy. The finding stands as count-based;
   actual PII enumeration not performed.
