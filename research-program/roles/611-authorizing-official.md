# NICE 611 — Authorizing Official / Designating Representative

**Source PDF:** `~/Documents/dod-cyber-pathways/611_Authorizing_Official.pdf`

**Role definition (NICE):** "Senior official or executive with the authority to formally assume responsibility for operating an information system at an acceptable level of risk to organizational operations (including mission, functions, image, or reputation), organizational assets, individuals, other organizations, and the Nation (CNSSI 4009)."

## Research-program relevance

The AO is the senior official who signs the ATO — the person who formally accepted the risk of operating the system NuClide just found exposed. Disclosure-routing-relevant: for federal/DoD targets, the AO is the ultimate accountable party when an inference endpoint, vector DB, or MLflow instance was deployed without an authorization boundary covering it. Heavy RMF/Information-Assurance/Legal KSA weighting means this role's language ("acceptable limits", "accreditation package", PII/PHI standards) is what disclosure reports to government targets should be written in to be actionable.
