# Disclaimer

The queries, fingerprints, and detection logic in this repository are provided **exclusively** for the following purposes:

- Authorized penetration testing and red team engagements with documented permission
- Defensive asset discovery (finding *your own* exposed infrastructure)
- Threat hunting and incident response
- Academic research and security education
- Coordinated vulnerability disclosure

## What this is not

This repository is **not** a license, an invitation, or a justification to:

- Access systems you do not own
- Probe infrastructure without explicit written authorization
- Extract data from third-party services
- Execute payloads against unconsenting targets

## Legal Context

In the United States, unauthorized access to computer systems is prohibited under the **Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (18 U.S.C. § 1030)**. Equivalent laws exist in most jurisdictions, including:

- UK: Computer Misuse Act 1990
- EU: Directive 2013/40/EU on attacks against information systems
- Canada: Criminal Code Section 342.1
- Australia: Cybercrime Act 2001

Civil liability under tort law, contract law (Terms of Service violations), and data protection regulations (GDPR, CCPA) may also apply.

## Responsible Disclosure

If your reconnaissance using these queries reveals exposed infrastructure belonging to a third party, please follow responsible disclosure:

1. **Do not access, log in, or extract data.** Fingerprinting from outside is one thing; authenticated access without permission is another.
2. **Identify the owner** via WHOIS, reverse DNS, certificate transparency, or organizational fingerprints.
3. **Report through their security contact** (`security.txt`, `/security`, security@org.tld) or a recognized coordinator (CERT/CC, your national CSIRT).
4. **Allow reasonable time** for remediation before any public discussion.

## Maintainer Position

The maintainer assumes no liability for misuse. The information catalogued here is publicly available, Shodan and equivalent search engines index it openly. Cataloguing what is *already discoverable* serves defenders. Using it to harm others does not.

**Use responsibly. Or don't use it at all.**
